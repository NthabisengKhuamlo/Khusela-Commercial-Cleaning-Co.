Why Regular Office Cleaning Boosts Staff Productivity

March 2025  |  5 min read  |  By Khusela Cleaning Co.

A clean office is not just about appearances. Research consistently shows that the physical environment in which people work has a direct and measurable impact on how well they perform. For business owners in Cape Town, investing in regular professional cleaning is one of the simplest and most cost-effective ways to improve your team's output.

The link between cleanliness and performance

Studies from the Princeton Neuroscience Institute found that physical clutter competes for your attention, reducing your ability to focus and process information. When employees work in a cluttered, dirty environment, their brains are constantly processing visual noise — even when they are not consciously aware of it. The result is reduced concentration, slower decision making and higher stress levels.

A clean, organised workspace removes that cognitive load. Employees can focus on the task in front of them rather than being subconsciously distracted by mess, dust and disorder around them.

What the numbers say

A study by the American Journal of Infection Control found that regularly cleaned offices showed a 46% reduction in sick days compared to offices cleaned infrequently. In South Africa, where businesses lose billions of rands annually to absenteeism, this is not a small number. Fewer sick days means more consistent output, fewer disruptions to projects and lower costs associated with covering absent staff.

Areas that make the biggest difference

High touch surfaces

Door handles, keyboard, phones, light switches and shared equipment are hotspots for bacteria and viruses. Regular disinfection of these surfaces is the single most effective way to reduce the spread of illness through your office.

Bathrooms and kitchens

Shared facilities have a disproportionate impact on how employees feel about their workplace. A dirty bathroom or kitchen signals to staff that the business does not care about their comfort and wellbeing. This affects morale, retention and even how your business is perceived by visiting clients.

Desks and workstations

The average office desk has 400 times more bacteria than a toilet seat. Regular professional cleaning of workstations, including beneath monitors and around keyboards, significantly reduces this bacterial load and the associated health risks.

The client impression factor

Beyond staff productivity, a clean office makes a powerful first impression on clients and visitors. Whether you are hosting a boardroom meeting, welcoming a new partner or receiving a supplier, the state of your office communicates your standards and professionalism before anyone says a word.

In Cape Town's competitive business environment, that first impression can be the difference between winning and losing a deal.

How often should you clean?

  • Daily: bins emptied, surfaces wiped, bathrooms and kitchens cleaned
  • Weekly: vacuuming, mopping, deep clean of shared areas
  • Monthly: windows, skirting boards, behind furniture, air vents
  • Quarterly: carpet cleaning, upholstery, deep kitchen clean

The right frequency depends on the size of your office, the number of staff and the nature of your business. A busy open plan office with 50 staff needs daily cleaning. A small three person studio might manage with three times a week.
