How Often Should You Professionally Clean Your Couch?

January 2025  |  5 min read  |  By Khusela Cleaning Co.

Your couch is probably the most used piece of furniture in your home or office. It absorbs sweat, skin cells, food particles, pet dander, dust mites and spills on a daily basis. Most people vacuum it occasionally and wipe up spills when they happen — but very few think about professional cleaning until there is a visible stain or a noticeable smell.

By then, the damage is often much deeper than the surface.

What is actually living in your couch

Research from environmental health studies consistently shows that upholstered furniture harbours significantly more bacteria, dust mites and allergens than hard surfaces. Dust mites in particular thrive in fabric environments — a single couch cushion can contain hundreds of thousands of them. For people with allergies or asthma, this is a serious health concern, not just a hygiene one.

Professional cleaning using hot water extraction or steam removes these contaminants from deep within the fabric, not just the surface layer.

How often should you clean?

Every 6 months

Households with pets or children

Higher traffic, more spills, more dander and more bacteria means more frequent cleaning is essential.

Once a year

Standard household

A couple or small family with no pets. Annual cleaning maintains freshness and extends the life of the fabric.

Every 3 months

Commercial and office sofas

High traffic shared seating in offices, waiting rooms and reception areas needs quarterly attention.

After every stay

Airbnb and rental properties

Upholstery in short term rentals should be deep cleaned between long stays or at least monthly.

Fabric types and what they need

Microfibre and synthetic fabrics

These are the most common and generally the easiest to clean. They respond well to hot water extraction and dry relatively quickly. Stains on synthetic fabrics are usually treatable if dealt with promptly.

Natural fabrics — linen, cotton, wool

Natural fabrics require more care. They can shrink or warp if cleaned incorrectly. Professional cleaning is particularly important here because incorrect products or techniques can permanently damage the fabric or alter its colour.

Leather and faux leather

Leather needs specialist conditioning as well as cleaning. Without conditioning, leather dries out, cracks and eventually deteriorates. Professional leather cleaning also identifies early signs of wear that can be treated before they become damage.

Velvet

Velvet is delicate and directional — incorrect cleaning can flatten the pile permanently. Always use a professional for velvet upholstery.

Signs your couch needs cleaning now

  • A musty or stale smell that does not go away after airing out
  • Visible stains or discolouration
  • Increased allergy symptoms when sitting on it
  • The fabric looks dull or flat compared to when you bought it
  • It has not been professionally cleaned in over a year
  • You have had guests, a party or a pet sleeping on it regularly

The cost of waiting too long

Deeply embedded stains, ingrained odours and deteriorated fabric are significantly more expensive to treat than regular maintenance cleaning. In many cases, a couch that has not been cleaned for several years cannot be fully restored. Regular professional cleaning extends the life of your furniture and protects your investment.

What to expect from a professional clean

A professional upholstery clean typically involves an initial inspection of the fabric type and condition, pre-treatment of any stains, hot water extraction or dry cleaning depending on the fabric, and a deodorising treatment. The couch will be damp after cleaning and needs a few hours to dry completely — we recommend leaving windows open and allowing good airflow.

The result is a couch that looks noticeably fresher, smells clean and is significantly more hygienic than before.
